Ann Reardon is a former Assistant Commonwealth’s Attorney, Assistant Attorney General of Virginia and Special Assistant United States Attorney who has appeared in court hundreds of times on a wide variety of criminal offenses and civil matters throughout the state. As a former prosecutor, she knows how cases are constructed from investigation through prosecution and is not afraid to challenge the government to fight for your rights. A successful trial attorney, she can handle any matter, criminal or civil, in court.

Ann grew up in Henrico County and has spent much of her life in the Richmond area. She is a 1981 graduate of the College of William and Mary and graduated from its law school, The Marshall – Wythe School of Law, in 1984.
